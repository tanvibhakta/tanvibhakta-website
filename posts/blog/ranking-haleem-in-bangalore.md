Ranking Haleem in Bangalore 

I cannot explain to you the importance Haleem has acquired in my life. But here are the facts:

1. When I can’t figure out what to eat, but I’m being picky and don’t have an appetite: haleem.

2. When I’m sick and need to get better: haleem.

3. When I didn’t trust myself to eat solids yet after [my wisdom tooth removal](): two point six kilograms of haleem, over the course of five days. No, I did not share. 

Good haleem should be

1. Rich 

2. Not too hot, but well rounded with spice 

3. Texturally interesting 

4. NO BONES

5. Not rely on the toppings for palatability.

The thing is, haleem is very hard to make, and one simply shouldn’t bother making it in small quantities. If you’re going to do it at home, I highly recommend following [Sohla El-Wayly’s](https://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/instant-pot-lamb-haleem) recipe - I doubled it in my 12 Quart instant pot. It was a LOT of work but highly worth it. If I live somewhere I cannot easily access haleem I see myself making and stocking large batches in the freezer. Luckily I live in Bangalore where over the last few years I’ve seen a lot more places sell them (year round!). This post is mostly a reminder to myself of what is possible and what my go tos should evolve to become.
