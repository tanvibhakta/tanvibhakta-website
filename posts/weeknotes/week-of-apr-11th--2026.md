## Work

Once again, something that was supposed to take two days to do took a whole week. Reasons?

1. It’s always the scope creep that gets you
2. Give people who might have opinions a chance to cut scope when on a timeline
3. Invest in a deploy process that takes not more than a few minutes. Ours currently takes 15 to 25; and it is a PAIN.
4. We are building a product that contains llms doing turn-based conversations; some (local) e2e tests[^1] therefore necessarily have to be driven by llms (or so I thought, a few months ago). This process also takes about 20 minutes to fully go through. But I’m not giving myself a hard time about this. The whole world is still figuring out best practices around building products that contain LLMS[^2]! I’m so excited to be on the cutting edge; I expect to bleed often.

Also [wrote up some reminders](/blog/reminders-for-engineers-using-llms) to myself while working with LLMs.

I’m very glad we’re moving to serverless. I’m sure this will come with its own issues that I will bemoan, but for now I am excited about

1. Not having to manage my own worktrees
2. Having exactly reproducible builds
3. Starting local in under thirty seconds
4. Having deploys take not more than a minute (we will still have to contend with ci though)
